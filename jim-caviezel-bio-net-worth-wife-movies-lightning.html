Jim Caviezel Biography

Jim Patrick Caviezel, an American actor, was born in Mount Vernon, Washington, on September 26, 1968.

He was one of James Caviezel, a chiropractor, and Margaret’s five children.

Margaret was a former theater actress. Sports and religion were his two main areas of interest as a child.

Jim Caviezel had exceptional basketball skills and aspired to one day play in the NBA.

At a very young age, he was also exposed to Christianity, routinely going to church with his family.

Jim’s basketball career and his aspirations of playing in the NBA came to an end when he suffered a foot injury during his sophomore year.

Soon after, he began to concentrate on acting.

Career Beginnings

Jim Caviezel began acting in plays in Seattle, Washington.

He was able to join the Screen Actors Guild because of a little role he had in the 1991 film My Own Private Idaho.

He then moved to Los Angeles in order to pursue a career as an actor.

In order to play Warren Earp in the 1994 film Wyatt Earp, he declined a scholarship given in 1993 to study acting at the Juilliard School in New York.

Later, he made guest appearances on episodes of The Wonder Years and Murder, She Wrote.

He made his acting debut in the 1998 World War II movie The Thin Red Line, directed by Terrence Malick, after appearing in G.I. Jane (1997).

In the 1999 American Civil War movie Ride with the Devil, he portrayed Black John, a Missouri bushwhacker.
