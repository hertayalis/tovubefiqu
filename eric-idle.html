Biography

Eric Idle was born in England on March 29, 1943. Member of the groundbreaking Monty Python comedy troupe who wrote the hit Broadway musical Spamalot. He first became known to American audiences in 1978 when NBC aired a mockumentary film, All You Need Is Cash, a parody of The Beatles movies, which he wrote and starred in. In 2002 he played against Martha Stewart and Charles Barkley on a celebrity edition of “Jeopardy!” and won. While studying English at Pembroke College at Cambridge he became the first president of the Cambridge University Footlights Club to allow membership to women. He later starred in the children’s television comedy series Do Not Adjust Your Set co-starring his future Python fellows Terry Jones and Michael Palin.
