Emma Stones Timeless Cashmere Peacoat Is on Sale for InStyle Readers

Publish date: 2024-07-02

Up until last month, I had never owned cashmere. But now that I’ve dipped my toe in the luxury wool pond, I understand the hype — even if my accountant isn’t quite so onboard. 

As if I needed any more of an excuse to hit “add to cart” on a new cashmere staple, Emma Stone stepped out in New York City sporting a Cashmere Peacoat of my dreams from California brand Jenni Kayne. The Poor Things actress, who just took home the Golden Globe for Best Actress in a Musical or Comedy Motion Picture, was photographed in early December rocking the outerwear style with a pair of baggy jeans and a black baseball cap. 

And while the khaki shade spotted on Stone is no longer available, the cashmere peacoat is still in stock in an equally (if not more so) stunning amber hue. The casual yet sophisticated jacket features a wool, polyester, and cashmere blend — with a few other materials mixed in there — to create that soft-to-the-touch effect. And you can snag the style for less by using our 15 percent-off code, JKFRIENDS15, at checkout.

Jenni Kayne Cashmere Peacoat

The peacoat style itself is classic and ultra-versatile. The design is simple with an unlined construction and button detailing, while the mid-thigh length feels equally elegant and casual. The jacket can easily elevate a more low-key look or tie together a more dressed up outfit. 

One shopper praised the style as “more amazing in person” thanks to its “gorgeous, rich color” and “soft and flowy” fabric, and another said that they “love everything” about it, including the “amazing quality and construction” and “classically timeless style.” A third customer raved that it was “the perfect addition” to their wardrobe, while noting that it’s “not too heavy yet heavy enough” and “looks very classy with jeans.” According to fellow reviewers, it fits slightly “oversized.” 

Jenni Kayne advises that you dry clean the cashmere peacoat so that it maintains its integrity. The fine nature of the yak and cashmere can cause pilling if you do not wash it correctly, and no one wants that.
